Working in the Baruch '60 Laboratory'

I worked at the Baruch '60' lab at my university, RPI, for some time while studying my undergraduate major. My personal interest in working here was to broaden both my own and humanity's understanding of biochemical photosynthesis.

While I spent some amount of time helping with this work, my primary focus while at the lab was helping author a paper on the binding properties of hemoglobin with oxygen. I also helped with synthesis of reagents necessary for the photosynthesis research. As part of my work with this and being a part of the laboratory team, I was required to read a significant amount of scientific literature and research on the topic and report my understanding to my lab professor.
