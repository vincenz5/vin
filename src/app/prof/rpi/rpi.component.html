Studying Chemical Engineering and Economics

I chose the biochemical and chemical engineering degree at RPI because of my passion for renewable energy. It made sense to me that to improve human energy systems, one should understand the properties of energy and engineering techniques for controlling it.

Also, I attained a minor in economics, which I found to be a solid companion for my interests to keep my skillset multidisciplinary and useful.

During my time at RPI, I was on the varsity rowing team, competing around the northeast US, as well as a travel team member and officer at RPI's EWB (Engineers Without Borders), and a member of the Alpha chapter of Theta Xi, a national fraternity primarily at engineering schools. As a travel team member at EWB, I was key in the system design of a rainwater collection and filtration system that was successfully installed by a town of about 300 people on Isla Popa II, a relatively remote island in Panama near Bocas del Toro.
