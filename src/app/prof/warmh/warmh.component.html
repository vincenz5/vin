Volunteering at Warm heart

I spent several months as a gap semester between high school and college volunteering at a childrens home in northern Thailand, near the town of Phrao. My duties were split between helping educating and taking care of a group of about 20 at-risk Thai hill tribe children and offering assistance with a pig farm biogester project. This project was led by the University of Chiang Mai as a pilot project for nation-wide pig farms. Traditionally, these farms dispose of their pig feces by dumping them into the rivers, contaminating water passing through populations and putting those people at risk of e coli and other pathogens. Through pressure and heat inside the biodigestor, not only are these pathogens eliminated but through an anaerobic process, methane gas is generated. This was made usable for both the pig farm's modified electric generator and local towns. The solid waste was dried in concrete pans and used for high quality fertilizer.

Incidentally, my engineering degree capstone project was the design of a much larger pig farm biodigestor, four years later.
