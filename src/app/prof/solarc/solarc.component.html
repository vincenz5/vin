Designing Systems at SolarCity

SolarCity brought me onto their design team in Roseville, CA to join a growing team of PV (photovoltaic) system designers. An average system design including:

  • reviewing site surveyor photos and paperwork for red flags and unusual needs of the building

  • designing the system specifications on AutoCAD, using SolarCity software paired with CAD

  • drawing out the installation three line diagram, specifying how the electrical wiring, stringing of the panels, tie in into the building's existing electric system, and electric safety mechanisms should be permitted according to and then installed

  • project management of the installation from the sale, through the preparation at the warehouse, up to and then after installation

  • a significant amount of quality control to ensure a perfect and accelerated customer experience, something our engineering department held, in my opinion, much better than any other solar installation firm I know of in the residential installation space

As our team worked inside the Roseville HQ, we had a strong relationship with the hundreds of SolarCity sales staff around us. A major responsibility of us being there was to work with these teams to improve their quality of sales expectations with their customers. Since the incentive of the sales staff is to increase their sales figures, they would often, at the beginning of my time at Roseville, offer customers greater expectations of savings from their system than was possible.

Factors causing a less favorable result than expecation included shading not taken into account, smaller roof size than assumed, less solar irradiance (solar energy arriving per unit of roof area) than assumed. Working with these sales teams, we succeeded in decreasing the difference between expectations and results, which led to a much higher rate of successful sales per initial lead.
